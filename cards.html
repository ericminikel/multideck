The Multideck includes a regular 52-card, four-color, four-pip set of playing cards (great for Poker and for southpaws), but you can also use the extra cards and markings to play some of of my favorite games. Here are some instructions from Adam:

Tichu
BGG wiki amazon Rio Grande
To play Tichu, add four extra cards: Dragon, Phoenix, Dog, and 1 (Mah Jong). Discard the Wizards.

Tichu is a partnered trick-taking/shedding game for four players, and is my favorite card game of all time. It's bit complex: it may take you half an hour to learn all the rules, and two or three hands before you really understand what's going on. But once you're over that, the learning curve is a real treat and the pacing is perfect. You can play for years and still be learning new strategies. Playing with the same partner (or same opponents) can build up a great dynamic over time.

Wizard (Designed by Ken Fisher)
BGG wiki amazon U.S. Games Systems
To play Wizard, use all 60 cards. Treat the Tichu cards as Jokers.

Wizard is a fun and flexible game. Because 60 is such a composite number, you can play with 3, 4, 5, or 6 players. It's pretty quick to pick up, and brand-new folks can play alongside seasoned pros (they'll lose, but should still have fun). I always play with the "screw the dealer" rule (once you hit the fourth round, the sum of the bids must not equal the number of tricks).

Lost Cities (Designed by Reiner Knizia)
BGG wiki amazon Rio Grande
To play Lost Cities, use all 60 cards. The fifth suit comprises the Aces, Wizards, and Tichu cards. It's labelled with Purple numbers and stars for the multipliers. The other four suits use the 2-9 for numbers and face-cards for multipliers.

Lost Cities is my favorite two-player card game (and one of my top two-players games of all time). In five minutes you'll understand the basic mechanics. In game one, you'll experience the tension that makes it fun. In game two, you'll realize the value of information. In game three, you'll realize the value of misinformation.

No Thanks (Designed by Thorsten Gimmler)
BGG wiki amazon Z-Man Games
To play No Thanks, pull out the cards with the white outlined numbers 3-35 and discard the rest. You also need some tokens; pennies work well.

No Thanks is the simplest fun game I know of. It can play 3-5 players; you can completely describe the rules to someone in 3-5 sentences, then enjoy playing for 3-5 hours. Make sure to keep the tokens hidden!
